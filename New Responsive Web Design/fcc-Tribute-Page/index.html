Nelson Mandela once a wise man

Nelson Mandela

The man who changed many south africans' lives!

Here's a recap of Mandela's some marking dates:

  • July 18, 1918: Nelson Rolihlahla Mandela is born in Mvezo in the Transkei (now the Eastern Cape Province) in South Africa.

  • 1944: Became a member of the African National Congress (ANC) in 1943, he co-founded the Youth League of the African National Congress with Oliver Tambo and Walter Sisulu.
  • 1949: joins the National Executive Committee of the ANC.

  • January 11, 1962: leaves the country to tour Africa in order to obtain financial support for the armed wing of the ANC (created in December 1961). He also underwent military training.

  • June 12, 1964: tried for sabotage and plotting to overthrow the regime, he was sentenced to life imprisonment in Robben Island prison, off Cape Town.

  • February 11, 1990: released from prison by the president of South Africa Frederik De Klerk, after twenty-seven years of imprisonment.

  • May 10, 1994: becomes the first black president of South Africa. He works for national reconciliation throughout his presidency.

  • 1993: receives the Nobel Peace Prize jointly with Frederik De Klerk.

  • 1994: publication of his autobiographical book A long road to freedom.

  • December 5, 2013: dies at his home in Johannesburg the age of 95.
